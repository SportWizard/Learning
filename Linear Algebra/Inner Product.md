# What is inner product?
Inner product builds off of [[Dot product]]. The result is in scalar

# Equation
$$
\begin{align}
V^\intercal U

&=

\begin{bmatrix}
v_1 \\ v_2 \\ \vdots \\ v_n
\end{bmatrix}^\intercal

\begin{bmatrix}
u_1 \\ u_2 \\ \vdots \\ u_n
\end{bmatrix}

\\
&=

\begin{bmatrix}
v_1 & v_2 & \cdots & v_n
\end{bmatrix}

\begin{bmatrix}
u_1 \\ u_2 \\ \vdots \\ u_n
\end{bmatrix}

\\
&=

v_1 \cdot u_1 + v_2 \cdot u_2 + \cdots + v_n \cdot u_n

\\
&=

\sum_{i=1}^n v_i u_i
\end{align}
$$
